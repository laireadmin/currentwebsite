What Should I Bring?

LAIRE is held on a camp in northern New Jersey, so pack as if you plan to go camping. (Tents are optional. Cabins and platform tents are provided on camp.)

Clothing: You will be active and in the woods. Pack clothing that is comfortable and you do not mind getting dirty. Generic dark colored clothing is good for NPC duty. Avoid clothing with logos or other designs unless easily covered. Check the weather forecast ahead of time and pack appropriately. Weather at the camp can change drastically and it is a good idea to pack extra clothes in case of rain, changes in temperature, etc. A good pair of waterproof boots will keep you dry and happy all weekend!

Sleeping: A good sleeping bag is extremely important! Stay warm during the Winter and on cool Spring, Summer, & Fall nights! Buildings are heated but space is limited and during the warmer seasons you may be sleeping in a tent.

Food & Drink: Food and drink is available for purchase thanks to volunteer community members who run the bar and the kitchen (non-alcoholic beverages only). This will run you about $25-$30, so remember to bring cash. If you have dietary restrictions please inform them or bring your own food and drink. It is recommended that you bring bottles of water. Any food brought to camp must be kept indoors (limited refrigerator space is available) and are not permitted in tents during the warm months. We are outdoors, and food and sugary drinks in a tent will attract unwanted wildlife.

A typical packing list should include:

  • a white headband
  • flashlight & batteries
  • pens
  • watch
  • bottled water
  • toothbrush & toothpaste
  • deodorant
  • towel
  • shampoo
  • soap
  • any needed medications
  • sleeping clothes
  • extra clothing for NPC duty
  • sweatpants/shirt
  • thermals
  • gloves, hat, and coat
  • waterproof hiking boots
  • extra socks (4-6 pairs recommended, especially in wet weather)
  • sleeping bag, pillow, and blankets
  • money and/or food (optional food services on camp require an additional fee)
  • costuming/armor
  • weapons/shield/spell packets
  • in-game items such as coins, alchemy, etc.
